# Dehydration

Dehydration occurs when you use or lose more fluid than you take in, and your body doesn't have enough water and other fluids to carry out its normal functions.

## Symptoms (Adults)

- Extreme thirst
- Less frequent urination
- Dark-colored urine
- Fatigue
- Dizziness
- Confusion

## Treatment

- Drink water or sports drinks containing electrolytes.
- Avoid caffeine and alcohol.
- Sucking on ice chips.

## Emergency Warning

Go to the ER if:

- You haven't urinated in 24 hours.
- You are disoriented or confused.
- You cannot keep fluids down due to vomiting.
- You have a rapid heartbeat or rapid breathing.
- Black or bloody stools.
